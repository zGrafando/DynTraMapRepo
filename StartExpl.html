This Web-application has but one goal and one goal only:
To exemplify and introduce anyone who should be unfamiliar,
to the Tramway-system of Zürich.
Within the adjacent pages available through the navigation on your
top-left corner, you will find a dynamic tramway-map alongside the
current timetable of the Citys tramway-system. Last but not least
you may find a topological display of the area the footage for the
tramway-map was created in, as well as some information concerning
the authors of this project, should any questions arrise.

The dynamic mapping works in the following manner.
Vidoe footage is taken from the back of the tram making for an otherwise
unfamiliar experience, since most tramway participants tend to naturally
look ahead. Furthermore, the length of a full tramway railroad is quite large
and takes about 1 to 1.5 hours to complete from start to finish.
Since we imagine footage of such length - provided all one sees is scenery of Zürich,
is appealing to none, the decision was made to speed up the footage by a factor of 5
This makes for a sort of zoom-out feeling while watching, almost like being draged
through and out of the city. What makes us call this dynamic mapping though?
Well, next to this footage, we display a logical map of the tramway-system harboring
a red dot, that is animated to wander along the course of the railway on which the tram
from which the footage was taken is currently positioned, so one can deduce the exact
geographical position of the scenery displayed at any given time.
